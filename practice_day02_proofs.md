## Warm-up (direct proofs)

**P1.** Prove that the sum of any two odd integers is even.

**P2.** Let a, b, c ∈ ℤ. Prove that if a | b and a | c, then a | (b + c).
*(Recall a | b means b = ak for some integer k.)*

---

## Contrapositive

**P3.** Let n ∈ ℤ. Prove that if n² is even, then n is even.
*(Hint: prove the contrapositive — "if n is odd, then n² is odd.")*

---

## Contradiction

**P4.** Prove that there is no largest integer.

**P5.** Prove that √3 is irrational.
*(Adapt the √2 argument. You may use the fact, provable as in P3, that if 3 | n² then 3 | n.)*

---

## Proof by cases

**P6.** Prove that for every integer n, the number n² + n is even.
*(Hint: split into n even and n odd.)*

---

## Functions (ties to today's topic)

**P7.** Let f : A → B and g : B → C be functions. Prove that **if g ∘ f is surjective, then g is surjective.**
*(This is the surjective companion to Q8 — and note it's g, not f, that must be onto.)*

---

## Disproof

**P8.** Decide whether the following is true, and prove or disprove it:
"For all integers n, if n² is divisible by 4, then n is divisible by 4."
